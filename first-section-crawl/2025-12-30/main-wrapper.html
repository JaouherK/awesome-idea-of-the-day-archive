The invoice cleared. $4,200 hits the account. Feels good for about an hour. Then the math starts. Rent is due. Quarterly taxes are coming. The credit card from last month's slow period. By the time the bills are paid, there's nothing left to save. Again. Good months disappear. Slow months hurt. Retirement stays a someday problem until someday arrives with nothing in the account. W-2 workers have HR. Someone sets up the 401k. Someone withholds taxes. Someone emails about the retirement match. Freelancers have a checking account and good intentions. Fifty-seven million Americans work for themselves now. Most are building businesses while their future sits untouched. Pay Yourself First builds what should already exist. Connect your bank and invoicing platforms. Every time a payment lands, money moves into the right buckets automatically. Tax reserves. Emergency fund. Retirement. Flush month? More flows to long-term savings. Slow month? Minimums stay protected. No transfers to remember. No discipline required. The sweet spot is freelancers earning $80K-$250K. Enough income to save meaningfully. Not enough to justify a $300/hour advisor. At $19-$49/month, this costs less than one advisory session and runs every day of the year. Grow through integrations with FreshBooks, Wave, HoneyBook. Partner with YouTube creators already teaching freelance finance. Answer the Reddit question that gets asked every week: "How much should I be saving for taxes?" Start with automated savings and tax reserves. Expand into quarterly reminders, health insurance guidance, investment recommendations shaped for irregular income. The gig economy scaled. The financial tools haven‚Äôt.
